01 / The Brief

Objectives & Methodology

The primary goal of this 4-hour sprint was to produce a bespoke family profile for Thomas Miller (b. 1842) and identify his parents using a combination of GRO indexes and local parish transcriptions.

We employed a systematic sweep of the 1851-1881 Censuses and cross-referenced results with the Norfolk Record Office's digital collection. All findings are documented below with standard genealogical citations.

02 / Evidence

Verified Records Located

Ref Code Year Record Type Details Located
REF-01 1842 Birth Registration Thomas Miller. District: Erpingham. Mother's Maiden Name: Howlett.
REF-02 1865 Marriage Cert Thomas Miller to Sarah Ann Smith. Father listed as Robert Miller (Laborer).
REF-03 1881 Census Return Household in Aylsham. Thomas (Head), 39, Ag Lab. 4 children listed.

Key Breakthrough

The identification of 'Howlett' as the mother's maiden name in the GRO index allows us to move back another generation to the marriage of Robert Miller and Elizabeth Howlett (1838).

03 / Conclusion

Summary of Sprint

We successfully pushed the Miller line back to 1810. We have confirmed the parental link for Thomas Miller, resolving the ambiguity caused by the three different "Thomas Millers" born in Norfolk in 1842.

Recommended Next Steps

  • Priority 1 Manorial Record Search: Investigate the Blickling Estate records (1830-1850) for mention of Robert Miller's tenancy.
  • Priority 2 DNA Triangulation: Check matches against descendants of the Howlett branch in Suffolk to confirm the 1838 marriage link.
